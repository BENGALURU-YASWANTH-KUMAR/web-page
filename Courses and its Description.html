B.Tech Courses

Computer Science Engineering
Focuses on the theoretical foundations of computation and practical techniques for their application in computer systems.
Electrical Engineering
The study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism, with a focus on power systems and electronics.
Mechanical Engineering
Deals with the principles of engineering, physics, and materials science for the design, analysis, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems.
Civil Engineering
Focuses on the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure projects such as roads, bridges, dams, and buildings.
Chemical Engineering
Combines principles of chemistry, physics, biology, mathematics, and economics to efficiently use, produce, transform, and transport chemicals, materials, and energy.
